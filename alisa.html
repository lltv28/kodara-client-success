04 — The solution

AI Studio Assistant for voice teachers

An AI trained on your 30+ years of methodology that handles the repetitive cognitive work — so teachers can focus on presence.

What it generates

  • Complete lesson plans: 15–20 min structured outlines with exercises, focus areas, and teaching cues per student
  • Warm-up sequences: pitch range, starting exercises, register progression, specific technical focuses
  • Practice assignments: step-by-step instructions, repetition counts, troubleshooting tips for common mistakes
  • Diagnostic assessments: identifies technical issues, suggests 3–5 exercises with rationale, modification options by skill level

What it tracks and builds

  • Song coaching: technical breakdown, why it's difficult, 2–3 targeted exercises, practice strategy per phrase
  • Progress tracking: documents improvements, flags recurring challenges, suggests next-step focuses, maintains continuity
  • Curriculum maps: logical skill progression, milestone checkpoints, repertoire suggestions aligned with development
  • Progress reports: translates technical improvements into understandable wins, includes practice recommendations, celebrates growth

05 — Differentiation

This isn't ChatGPT

Generic AI doesn't know your methodology, exercises, somatic approach, or how to think like a master teacher.

Generic AI says...

  • "Practice scales to improve range"
  • "Relax your throat and breathe deeply"
  • "Do lip trills as a warm-up"
  • "Sing the passage slowly, then speed up"
  • No nervous system or trauma awareness
  • No pedagogical reasoning or "why"

Alisa's AI says...

  • Prescribes honeypot, primal sounds, energetic lines of force, mixed belt technique
  • Integrates nervous system regulation before technique
  • Suggests yoga/breath work when student needs grounding
  • Provides the "why" behind every exercise recommendation
  • Uses Alisa's specific metaphors, imagery, and teaching language
  • Accounts for voice type, age, style differences, and emotional blocks
  • Speaks teacher-to-teacher with specific exercise names and terminology

The manual alternative

Teachers currently spend 5–7.5 hours/week planning for 30 students. Hiring an assistant costs $20–$30/hr but can't replicate pedagogical expertise. Pre-made curriculum doesn't customize. Most teachers have tried templates, notebooks, or systems but still end up customizing everything. They either sacrifice personalization (burnout prevention) or sacrifice income/personal time (burnout guarantee).
