# Facebook Posts Generator — Instructions for AI

Instructions for creating a warm-audience Facebook post campaign for any Kodara client, given their offer reference material.

**Reference implementations:** `maverick-facebook-posts.md` (mixed format, 10 posts) and `maverick-facebook-background-posts.md` (short engagement posts) in this folder.

---

## 1. Overview

This is a **10-post Facebook campaign** that:

- Targets the client's existing network (friends, followers, group members who know them)
- Alternates between long-form text posts and short color-background posts
- Builds belief before introducing the product (same arc as the email sequence)
- Uses comment/DM CTAs — no links
- Posts every 2-3 days, interleaved with the client's regular content
- Can be output as a single markdown file (`.md`)

---

## 2. Source Material

The Facebook posts draw from the same offer file. Key sections:

```
FROM THE OFFER FILE:
- Context sections 1-8 (client, audience, problems, credibility, market)
- Product concept (what it does, who it's for, differentiators)
- Belief Ladder (pain, pointing, selling)
- Pricing (for product intro posts)
- Social media talking points (if present in the offer — use as direct reference)
```

If the offer file includes a "Social Media Talking Points" section, **use those as the foundation** and expand into the 10-post format below.

---

## 3. Post-by-Post Structure

### Campaign Arc

| Phase | Posts | Purpose |
|-------|-------|---------|
| **Awareness** | 1-4 | Build problem awareness with engagement hooks |
| **Solution** | 5-7 | Introduce the approach and the product |
| **Reinforcement** | 8-10 | Drive action through pain reminders and belief statements |

### Format Pattern

The 10 posts alternate between two formats:

- **Odd posts (1, 3, 5, 7, 9):** Long-form text posts (200-400 words)
- **Even posts (2, 4, 6, 8, 10):** Short color-background posts (1-2 sentences)

This creates variety in the feed and boosts engagement between heavier content posts.

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day** | Day 1 |
| **Focus** | Desire — opportunity awareness |
| **Content** | Lead with the biggest market stat. Show the gap. Use the client's credibility to frame it ("I've been doing [X] for [Y] years and I've never seen..."). End with a comment CTA asking what they're seeing. |

### Post 2: The Dream Question (Background)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day** | Day 3 |
| **Focus** | Desire — gets people dreaming |
| **Content** | One question that makes the reader imagine their ideal outcome. "What would [specific positive result] change for [your situation]?" |
| **CTA** | Implicit — the question itself drives comments |

### Post 3: The Bottleneck (Long-form)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day** | Day 5 |
| **Focus** | Pain — validation of the struggle |
| **Content** | Name the real problem everyone knows but nobody says publicly. Use specific numbers (hours, costs, volume). Show the cascade of consequences. Validate that it's a systemic issue, not a personal failure. |
| **CTA** | "How many [specific metric] is your [team/business] dealing with? Comment below or message me." |

### Post 4: The Engagement Prompt (Background)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day** | Day 7 |
| **Focus** | Engagement — gets people sharing numbers |
| **Content** | One specific question that invites people to share a number or data point related to the pain. "Real talk — how many [hours/dollars/attempts] does your [team] spend on [problem]? Drop a number below" |
| **CTA** | Implicit — asks for a number |

### Post 5: The Early Movers (Long-form)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day** | Day 9 |
| **Focus** | Pointing — the general approach (before naming the product) |
| **Content** | Reference that some people in the audience's world are getting ahead. Use specific result numbers (3-5x improvement, time savings, etc.). Create FOMO without naming the product yet. End with offer to show how it works. |
| **CTA** | "Send me a message and I'll walk you through it." |

### Post 6: The Priority Question (Background)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day** | Day 11 |
| **Focus** | Desire — reveals what they care about |
| **Content** | Ask what their #1 priority would be if the bottleneck were removed. "If you could get one [thing] fully [resolved] this year — [option A, B, C, or D] — what would it be?" |
| **CTA** | Implicit — multiple choice drives comments |

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day** | Day 13 |
| **Focus** | Product intro — origin story |
| **Content** | Tell the honest story of why the product was built. Include the limitation that forced the creation ("we had to turn away X people"). Introduce the product by name. Differentiate from generic alternatives. List key capabilities. |

### Post 8: The Missed Opportunity (Background)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day** | Day 15 |
| **Focus** | Pain — reflection prompt |

| **CTA** | Implicit — triggers reflection and comments |

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Day** | Day 17 |

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|

| **CTA** | Implicit — agree/disagree drives engagement and shares |

## 4. Tone and Voice Rules

1. **Personal and authentic.** These are posts from a real person's Facebook profile, not a brand page.
2. **No hashtags.** This is warm audience content, not discovery content.
3. **No links.** Every CTA is "comment below" or "send me a message/DM."
4. **No emojis** unless the client's natural posting style uses them.
5. **Short paragraphs.** Especially for long-form posts — one thought per line.
6. **Use the client's voice.** Their terminology, their industry language.
7. **Be specific.** Numbers, scenarios, dollar amounts. Never vague.
8. **Background posts are 1-2 sentences max.** They're designed for Facebook's colored-background text post format.

|------|-----|--------|-------|

| 3 | Day 5 | Long-form | Pain — validation of the struggle |

| 5 | Day 9 | Long-form | Pointing — the approach |

**Notes:**
- Posts 1-5 build belief before the product is introduced in Post 7
- Background posts boost engagement between the heavier long-form posts
- Total campaign: 19 days
- These should be interleaved with the client's normal posting (not the only thing they post)

## 6. Content Mapping Checklist

Ensure these elements from the offer appear across the 10 posts:

### Posts 1-4 (Awareness)
- [ ] Biggest market stat or opportunity number
- [ ] Full pain description with specific hours/costs
- [ ] Audience-specific frustrations named
- [ ] At least one engagement question tied to a real metric

### Posts 5-7 (Solution)
- [ ] Early adopter results with specific numbers (time savings, volume increase)
- [ ] Origin story — why the product was built
- [ ] Product name introduced
- [ ] Key differentiators from generic alternatives
- [ ] Top capabilities listed

### Posts 8-10 (Reinforcement)
- [ ] Cost of inaction with specific dollar estimates
- [ ] A specific missed-opportunity scenario
- [ ] Core belief statement that's shareable

### Throughout
- [ ] Client's credibility referenced naturally (years of experience, achievements)
- [ ] Specific numbers from the offer (not rounded or generalized)
- [ ] Comment/DM CTAs only (no links)
- [ ] Mix of 5 long-form + 5 background posts

## 7. Naming and File Conventions

- Combined file: `{clientname}-facebook-posts.md` (lowercase, hyphens)
- Background posts only (optional separate file): `{clientname}-facebook-background-posts.md`

## 8. Step-by-Step Build Process

1. **Read the offer file** completely. Identify: client name, product name, audience, all sections.
2. **Check for existing social media talking points** in the offer. If present, use as foundation.
3. **Identify the strongest opening stat** for Post 1.
4. **Write all 10 posts** in order, alternating long-form and background format.
5. **Verify completeness** against the checklist in Section 6.
6. **Review tone** — every post should sound like it was written by the client on their personal Facebook.
7. **Optionally create a separate background-posts-only file** with just the 5 short posts.

---

## 9. Adaptation Notes

### Platform variations
- **Facebook**: As described above — long-form + color background alternating
- **LinkedIn**: Same structure but slightly more professional tone. Replace "DM" with "send me a message." Background posts become short text-only posts.
- **Instagram**: Long-form posts become carousel captions. Background posts become Stories polls or question stickers.

### Different audience warmth levels
- **Very warm** (past clients): More casual, reference shared experiences
- **Warm** (followers/connections): As described — personal but sets context
- **Group audience** (Facebook group members): Slightly more context-setting, can reference group topics

### Shorter campaigns
If fewer than 10 posts are needed, prioritize:
1. Post 1 (opportunity), 3 (pain), 5 (early movers), 7 (product reveal), 9 (cost of inaction)
2. Add background posts between as budget/time allows

### Multiple audiences
If the offer has two audiences, create separate post campaigns for each. The client can post them to different audiences using Facebook's audience targeting or in different groups.
