01 — The Pain

The growth ceiling: they can’t scale past what one person can touch

Phase 1 clients are hands-on service providers in their 50s–60s with 5–10 employees. They’re turning down work, watching quality slip, and can’t grow—because the business is capped by their personal capacity. They’ve never been shown structure that works for an 8-person shop, and every time someone else handles something, quality drops.

The growth ceiling

  • Turning down clients, saying no to opportunities—not because the work is bad, but because the business can’t scale past one person
  • Every time someone else handles something, quality drops—so they take it back and the ceiling stays
  • They want to grow but believe they’re the only one who can maintain their standard

Failed hires + leadership skepticism

  • They’ve tried hiring managers—the person walked into zero infrastructure and floundered. The owner blames the hire, not the environment
  • They think “management” and “structure” is corporate BS for companies with HR departments, not their 8-person shop
  • Nobody has ever shown them structure that works for how THEY actually operate

Team can’t think independently

  • Everything lives in the owner’s head—no SOPs, no documented standards, no decision context shared with the team
  • Employees do tasks but don’t carry mental load. They can’t anticipate problems or make judgment calls because they’re missing the context
  • The owner wants people who THINK, not just follow—but there’s nothing for them to think WITH
“I Need That” Moment

“You turned down a contract last month—not because you couldn’t do the work, but because you didn’t have the capacity. And by capacity, you mean YOU. There’s nobody else who can run it at your level. You’ve got 8 employees but the business literally can’t grow past what you personally can touch. You tried hiring someone—they walked in, saw no structure, no expectations, and floundered. You took the work back because the quality dropped. And the ceiling stayed exactly where it was.”

02 — The Solution

You talk. We build. Your standards transfer.

Bulletproof COO extracts the owner’s standards, judgment, and way of doing things through a few guided conversations, then builds a structure their team can execute at the owner’s level—no existing infrastructure required.

How it works (the process)

  • 3–5 guided voice conversations over 1–2 weeks (15–30 min each). The owner talks about their business the way they’d explain it to a sharp new hire. That’s the input
  • Connects to whatever tools they already use (email, CRM, scheduling software). If all they have is a phone and email, that’s enough. No Slack needed. No meeting recordings needed
  • The system listens, organizes, and builds using Simone’s proven methodology. No forms, no questionnaires, no writing required from the owner
  • Monthly updates keep the system current as the business evolves. New hires, role changes, new services—the system adapts instead of collecting dust

What they walk away with (outcomes)

  • Role expectations and decision-making boundaries for every employee—so the team stops coming to the owner for every answer
  • Their standards enforceable, not just documented—the actual bar the team has to hit, captured so quality stays at the owner’s level without them watching
  • Context that makes the team THINK—not just follow steps, but understand the WHY so they anticipate problems and make judgment calls the owner would make
  • Hire-ready onboarding + weekly team rhythm + delegation guidelines—the next employee has a playbook, the team has clarity, and the owner stops being the ceiling

03 — Differentiation

Built for businesses with zero existing infrastructure

Every other approach assumes…

  • You already have documentation to work from
  • Your team uses collaboration tools (Slack, PM software)
  • You hold structured meetings with notes and action items
  • You have 40+ hours to fill out templates and write SOPs
  • The problem is knowledge (it’s not—they’ve read the books)

Bulletproof COO starts from reality…

  • Starts with what they actually have: their knowledge, their voice, their way of doing things. No existing documentation required
  • Works with whatever tools they use—even if it’s just email and a phone. No Slack, no PM software needed
  • Guided conversations replace forms and templates. They talk, the system builds
  • Built on Simone’s 10+ years of turning owner-dependent businesses into self-running teams—specific methodology, not generic advice
  • Doesn’t require learning “leadership” or management frameworks. No corporate HR language. Just the owner’s way of doing things, made transferable to their team

What it costs to keep doing nothing

  • Failed hire: $50K–$100K in salary, lost time, and re-hiring. The person walks into chaos with no structure, no expectations, and no chance of success
  • DIY systems: 40–60 hours the owner doesn’t have, pulling them away from billable work that pays the bills
  • Consultant: $10K–$20K for a plan based on what the owner said (not what actually happens). The binder sits on a shelf
  • Doing nothing: the business stays capped by one person’s capacity, growth stalls, quality slips from being spread too thin, and the ceiling stays exactly where it is
