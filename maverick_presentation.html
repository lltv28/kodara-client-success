01 — Context

What communities do every funding cycle

The repetitive work

  • Filling out the same types of grant forms: project descriptions, budgets, outcomes, timelines
  • Writing organizational background sections from scratch each time
  • Creating budgets with line-item breakdowns and matching fund calculations
  • Drafting letters of support for every application
  • Preparing Band Council resolutions for funding authorization
  • Building evaluation frameworks with measurable outcomes
  • Adapting the same content for different funders (ISC, provincial, corporate, foundation)
  • Each grant requires 15–40 hours of work — repeated monthly, quarterly, annually

What they already do well

  • Know their community's needs intimately
  • Understand what programs work and what infrastructure is missing
  • Have relationships with Elders, community members, and partner organizations
  • Can articulate the problem and the vision face-to-face
  • Have often already run smaller versions of projects
  • Years of operational experience in community programs

02 — Frustrations

What they've tried — and why it hasn't worked

Alternatives they've tried

  • Hiring external grant writers — $1,000–$5,000+ per application, often upfront, no guarantee of success, writers don't understand community context
  • DIY with ChatGPT — generic output, no knowledge of Indigenous-specific funding streams, hallucinations, doesn't know funder priorities
  • Hiring staff — $50,000–$70,000/year for a grant coordinator, hard to recruit in remote areas, still steep learning curve
  • Outsourcing to consultants — expensive hourly rates ($50–$150/hour), 20–40 hours per grant

Their core frustrations

  • Limited administrative capacity across most communities
  • Struggle with basic paperwork completion and online portal literacy
  • Can't keep up with application volume despite qualifying for funding
  • Short-notice deadlines (2–4 weeks) make thorough applications nearly impossible
  • External writers lack cultural context and understanding of Indigenous governance
  • Generic AI produces unusable output for Indigenous-specific programs

05 — Differentiation

This isn't ChatGPT

Generic AI doesn't know ISC from ESDC, can't write a Band Council resolution, and hallucinates deadlines. This was built for Indigenous grants.

Generic AI says...

  • "Write a project description for your grant" — no funder context
  • Hallucinated deadlines and program names
  • No knowledge of Indigenous-specific funding streams
  • Generic budget templates that don't match portal requirements
  • Doesn't know Treaty territory distinctions or governance structures
  • Can't flag common disqualification errors
  • No cultural protocol guidance

Iskotew's AI says...

  • Pre-loaded with 200+ Indigenous-specific funding programs including eligibility, priorities, and evaluation rubrics
  • Trained on successful First Nations grant applications across all sectors
  • Understands Treaty territory context, on-reserve vs off-reserve distinctions
  • Knows funder-specific language: ISC wants "capacity building," foundations want "community-led," corporate wants "sustainable impact"
  • Flags common disqualification errors: missing CRA numbers, incorrect legal names, budget misalignment
  • Includes cultural protocol guidance: when to mention Elders, how to frame land-based teachings
  • Outputs match exact funder templates and portal requirements

The manual alternative

Hiring a grant writer: $1,500–$5,000 per application, 3–6 week turnaround, often doesn't understand community context. DIY with staff: 20–40 hours per application, high error rate, generic ChatGPT produces unusable output. Outsourcing to Maverick's team: 10% commission (works well but capacity-limited, can only handle 15–20 clients at a time).

08 — Package

What's included — and what's not

Grant Search Prompt Library

20+ pre-built prompts for finding federal, provincial, corporate, and foundation funding specific to Indigenous communities. Already exists in internal procedures, repackaged as a user resource.

Funder Database

Searchable list of 200+ Indigenous-specific funding programs with eligibility criteria, deadlines, priorities, and direct links. The core value-add that justifies why this isn't just ChatGPT.

Video Walkthrough Series

8 short videos (5–10 minutes each) showing how to use the AI for different grant types: infrastructure, youth programs, emergency services, cultural projects, economic development. Record once, reuse forever.

Monthly Group Coaching Call

60-minute live Q&A with Maverick and team. Review AI outputs, troubleshoot applications, discuss funder priorities. Builds community, increases retention, low time cost per user.

Grant Application Templates

Band Council resolution template, letter of support template, budget worksheet, project timeline framework. Already exist in internal processes, formatted for client use.

Success Story Library

10–15 examples of successful grant applications (redacted/anonymized) showing what funders approved, with annotations explaining why they worked. Builds trust, shows proof, educates users.

What to leave out

  • Full 1-hour AI grant writing course ($47–$97 standalone) — keep separate as low-ticket entry product or upsell for deeper AI training
  • Done-for-you grant writing service (10% commission model) — this is the premium tier, don't cannibalize it; position AI as DIY, done-for-you as upgrade
  • Emergency services training programs — completely different business line; including it would confuse positioning and dilute the grant-focused value
The complete product

First Nations Grant AI is a complete grant application system that generates funder-ready proposals, budgets, and supporting documents in under 3 hours, backed by a database of 200+ Indigenous funding programs, monthly expert coaching, and proven templates used to secure millions in funding. What the buyer walks away with: unlimited grant applications per month, access to all funding program data, monthly live support, and every template needed to submit complete applications without hiring external writers.
