3 Pointing · Day 5
Subject
The bottleneck nobody sees

Hey [First Name],

I want to share something I've come to believe after 30 years of teaching voice.

The thing that separates a good voice teacher from a great one isn't talent. It isn't credentials. It isn't even ears.

It's personalization.

Knowing that this student needs a longer warm-up focused on release before they can access their upper register. Knowing that student responds to somatic cues better than anatomical ones. Knowing exactly which exercise to pull out when a 62-year-old mezzo is losing flexibility in her middle voice.

That personalization is your superpower. It's why students stay with you for 5, 7, even 10 years.

But it also takes enormous mental energy. And there's no shortcut. You can't buy a pre-made curriculum for $300 and hand it to every student. That defeats the entire purpose. You can't hire an assistant for $25/hour because they don't have your pedagogical brain.

So you do it all yourself. And the work that makes you irreplaceable is the same work that traps you in an hourly model with a hard ceiling.

I've been asking myself: what if there were a way to keep the personalization but dramatically reduce the time it takes to produce it?

Not a template. Not a generic tool. Something that actually understands vocal pedagogy the way you and I do.

If you've ever tried to systematize your lesson planning and been frustrated by how inadequate the options are, reply and tell me what you tried. I've been through the same thing.
