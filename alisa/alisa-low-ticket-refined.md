### Audience B: Voice Teachers (B2B)

| Indicator | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|

**B2B Market Score: 28/40 — Strong.** Higher pain, clearer ROI, but smaller addressable market. Low-ticket products here should function as buyer qualification and upsell triggers to the $397 AI Studio Assistant.

### Critical Insight

These are two completely different funnels selling to two completely different people with two completely different psychologies. The current product list mixes them, which creates confusion.

**Recommendation:** Treat singers and teachers as separate product lines with separate funnels. Never put a singer through a teacher's funnel or vice versa. The cross-audience products (17-20) should be built with a primary audience in mind and adapted for the secondary one — not positioned as "both."

## Value Equation Lens: What Makes Low-Ticket Convert

For low-ticket ($7-$47), the value equation shifts:

```
                Dream Outcome  x  Perceived Likelihood
Value =    ──────────────────────────────────────────────
                Time Delay  x  Effort & Sacrifice
```

- **Dream Outcome** matters, but it's a *micro-dream* — one specific, tangible result, not transformation.
- **Perceived Likelihood** is driven by specificity and authority, not case studies.
- **Time Delay** must approach zero. They should get a result within hours or days, not weeks.
- **Effort & Sacrifice** must be minimal. Press play. Follow the steps. Done.

**The low-ticket rule:** Solve one sharp problem completely, in under 30 minutes of effort, with a result they can feel the same day.

## Scarcity & Urgency: Applied to Low-Ticket

Low-ticket products don't typically need scarcity — they convert on impulse and perceived value. But two mechanisms are worth using:

### 1. Launch Pricing (Urgency)
For initial launches: "First 100 buyers get this at $[launch price]. After that, it goes to $[regular price]." Use this for Products #5, #6, #14, and #17 where the regular price has room to increase.

### 2. Bonus Scarcity
"Buy this week and I'll include [specific bonus]." Works well for Products #10 (add a bonus song coaching video), #11 (add a 15-minute Q&A recording), and #15 (add an 11th bonus lesson plan).

### 3. Bundle Deadlines
"The [Name] Bundle is available through [date]. After that, these go back to individual pricing." Use for seasonal promotions combining 3-4 products.
