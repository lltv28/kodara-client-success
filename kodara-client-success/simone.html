01 — The Pain

The identity trap: they want freedom but can’t let go

Phase 1 clients are hands-on service providers in their 50s–60s with 5–10 employees. They hate managing, have never been trained to lead, and believe nobody can do it at their level. They want a team that works without them—but every time someone tries to step up, they take the work back.

The paradox

  • They say they want freedom, but being the indispensable leader IS their identity
  • Every time someone steps up, they take the work back—“nobody does it at my level”
  • They’d feel at a loss for what to do if someone actually took over—but they won’t say this directly

Failed hires

  • They’ve tried hiring managers—the person walked into zero infrastructure, no documentation, no vision, and floundered
  • Either the hire went stagnant or the owner held on so tight they couldn’t operate
  • The failure was the environment, not the person—but the owner blames the hire

No infrastructure exists

  • Everything lives in their heads—no SOPs, no documented processes, no meeting notes, no recordings
  • They trained their team ad hoc. What they call “meetings” is running into an employee in the hallway
  • They don’t have time to build systems AND they believe they don’t have time to run systems
“I Need That” Moment

“You finally found someone you thought could take some of the weight off. Three weeks in, they’re in your office ten times a day because nothing is written down, no expectations are clear, and they have no idea how you want things done. After two months you’re doing their job AND yours. After four months, one of you quits. You tell yourself ‘you just can’t find good people’—but the truth is, you never gave them a chance to succeed.”

02 — The Solution

You talk. We build. No documentation needed.

Bulletproof COO extracts what’s in the owner’s head through a few guided conversations, then builds their complete operating system from scratch—no existing infrastructure required.

How it works (the process)

  • 3–5 guided voice conversations over 1–2 weeks (15–30 min each). The owner talks about their business the way they’d explain it to a sharp new hire. That’s the input
  • Connects to whatever tools they already use (email, CRM, scheduling software). If all they have is a phone and email, that’s enough. No Slack needed. No meeting recordings needed
  • The system listens, organizes, and builds using Simone’s proven methodology. No forms, no questionnaires, no writing required from the owner
  • Monthly updates keep the system current as the business evolves. New hires, role changes, new services—the system adapts instead of collecting dust

What they walk away with (outcomes)

  • Role expectations for every employee: what they own, what they don’t, and clear decision-making boundaries—so the team stops coming to the owner for every answer
  • Their standards documented: the “way we do things” captured and organized so anyone can follow it without guessing or asking the owner
  • A simple weekly team rhythm (20 min) that replaces hallway conversations. Delegation guidelines for what stays with the owner and what goes to the team
  • Hire-ready onboarding package: the next employee has a real playbook from day one instead of spending their first month reading the owner’s mind

03 — Differentiation

Built for businesses with zero existing infrastructure

Every other approach assumes…

  • You already have documentation to work from
  • Your team uses collaboration tools (Slack, PM software)
  • You hold structured meetings with notes and action items
  • You have 40+ hours to fill out templates and write SOPs
  • The problem is knowledge (it’s not—they’ve read the books)

Bulletproof COO starts from reality…

  • Starts with what they actually have: their knowledge, their voice, their way of doing things. No existing documentation required
  • Works with whatever tools they use—even if it’s just email and a phone. No Slack, no PM software needed
  • Guided conversations replace forms and templates. They talk, the system builds
  • Built on Simone’s 10+ years of turning owner-dependent businesses into self-running teams—specific methodology, not generic advice
  • Includes Simone’s coaching perspective: flags when owners are holding onto work that isn’t theirs, and shows them specifically how to let go

What it costs to keep doing nothing

  • Failed hire: $50K–$100K in salary, lost time, and re-hiring. The person walks into chaos with no structure, no expectations, and no chance of success
  • DIY systems: 40–60 hours the owner doesn’t have, pulling them away from billable work that pays the bills
  • Consultant: $10K–$20K for a plan based on what the owner said (not what actually happens). The binder sits on a shelf
  • Doing nothing: the business stays dependent on the owner, every opportunity requires them, and the ceiling gets lower every year

04 — Belief Ladder

Three levels of buyer belief (Phase 1)

Level 1 — The Paradox
  • You say you want a team that works without you. But every time someone steps up, you take the work back—because nobody does it at your level. Both things can’t be true
  • Your last hire failed not because they weren’t good—but because there was nothing for them to work with. No docs, no expectations, no structure. You set them up to fail
  • What happens if you can’t show up tomorrow? Not retirement—illness, opportunity, or just wanting a week off. Right now your business can’t answer that question
Level 2 — Pointing
  • Every approach has failed because it requires the most overloaded person (you) to do the work. Books, templates, consultants—all need 40 hours you don’t have
  • What if someone just listened to how you explain your business and built the system from that? You already do this every time you train someone—it just never gets captured
  • The result: a team that knows what’s expected, can make decisions within their role, and doesn’t need you for every answer. Your next hire walks into infrastructure, not chaos
Level 3 — Selling
  • 3–5 short conversations over 1–2 weeks. You talk about your business. The system captures, organizes, and builds your complete operating system. No writing, no forms, no software to learn
  • Built on Simone’s 10+ years turning owner-dependent businesses into self-running teams. The system knows where owners hold on too tight, where teams need clarity, and where handoffs break down
  • Monthly updates keep the system alive. $200/month ongoing ensures it grows with the business instead of becoming another binder on a shelf
